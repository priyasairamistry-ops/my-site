Most advisory engagements deliver one of two things: a commercially compelling recommendation that does not survive contact with the technical team, or a technically rigorous assessment that cannot land with the business. The value of working with someone who holds both dimensions is not that you get two things — it is that you get one thing that actually works.

I am a technology advisor operating across telco, tech, and media and entertainment. My work spans two core outcomes: helping organisations deploy agentic AI where it will drive measurable commercial impact, and helping them reduce the technology cost base without the programme unravelling mid-delivery.

Over the years I have worked across a wide range of engagements — Salesforce implementations where I sat on the business stakeholder side, IT cost-out programmes as Programme Manager, managed service procurement, content management system discovery, and research-led strategy work. That breadth is not accidental. It is what allows me to recognise structural patterns that specialists in a single domain consistently miss.

The through-line is translation — taking ideas, mandates, or instincts that are still unformed and converting them into structured, credible, actionable output. Whether that is a board-ready AI business case, a vendor rationalisation programme, or a technology strategy that a CTO and a CFO will both sign off on.

I work with SMEs and large enterprises. The scale of the engagement differs; the standard of the work does not.

Services

Three areas.
Specific outcomes.

01

Agentic AI for Sales & Marketing

The question is not whether AI can automate parts of your commercial process — it almost certainly can. The question is which parts, in what sequence, and with what safeguards — given your current technology estate, your data maturity, and your team's capacity to absorb change.

I work with organisations to move from AI interest to AI readiness: identifying the highest-leverage use cases within Sales and Marketing, validating them against what existing systems can actually support, and producing proposals with enough technical depth to survive scrutiny and enough commercial clarity to secure sign-off.

Typical outputs

  • Use case validation workshops
  • Technical scoping documents
  • Vendor-agnostic recommendations
  • AI readiness assessments
  • Board-level business cases
02

Technology Strategy Advisory

A technology strategy that is not commercially grounded is a roadmap with no destination. I help leadership teams in telco, tech, and media and entertainment make technology decisions that connect directly to the outcomes they are accountable for — revenue growth, cost reduction, or competitive differentiation.

My focus areas are Sales Technology, Marketing Technology, and broader AI strategy. I bring analytically rigorous framing to the problem and delivery-tested pragmatism to the recommendation — the combination that produces strategy documents that do not gather dust.

Typical outputs

  • Technology landscape assessments
  • CRM and MarTech strategy
  • Make-or-buy analysis
  • Programme shaping
  • Stakeholder alignment workshops
03

IT Cost Reduction

The gap between the cost saving in the business case and the cost saving on the P&L is a structural problem — and it starts at the point of scoping. Vendors present optimistic figures; internal teams do not always have the independent perspective to push back; and the programme gets shaped around assumptions that do not survive the first quarter of delivery.

I work with organisations to build the cost reduction case properly from the start: establishing the real baseline, identifying where the exposure sits, and structuring a programme that is credible enough to withstand challenge and specific enough to actually deliver.

Typical outputs

  • IT cost baseline and benchmarking
  • Vendor and contract rationalisation
  • Managed service procurement support
  • Programme management
