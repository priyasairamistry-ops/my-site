Design notes

Option 1 — Mark + Wordmark

The nested diamond mark references the intersection of business and technology — two overlapping dimensions distilled into a single geometric form. The amber fill on the inner diamond, with a dark void at centre, creates depth without complexity. Works as a standalone mark at small sizes (favicon, social avatar).

Option 2 — Typographic

The italic initials PSM in Cormorant Garamond carry the visual weight — "P" in amber establishes the accent anchor, "SM" in the body colour creates contrast within the mark itself. The horizontal rule and spaced name below give the logo structure without needing an icon. Strongest at larger sizes — decks, proposals, headers.

Colour logic

Amber (#c09050) is consistent across both dark and light backgrounds — it's the brand constant. Text switches between #ede8df on dark and #1c1a17 on light. Both logos are built as inline SVGs so colour adapts cleanly to any background.

Recommendation

Option 1 gives you a distinctive mark that can be used in isolation — useful for social media, favicons, and small-format applications. Option 2 works better as a document or presentation signature. Both use the same type system as the website (Cormorant Garamond + DM Sans) so they sit natively within the brand.
