# Rahul Khanna — Angel Investor Review

**Verdict: Right category. Right tone. Zero proof. I'm passing for now.**

---

## First impression

The design signals premium and the positioning signals specificity. "Technology advisory for telco, tech, and media" is narrow enough to be credible — she's not trying to be everything to everyone. The dark aesthetic reads as confident rather than corporate. I've seen a lot of consultant websites. This one is better than most on first impression.

The headline is legitimately good. "From ambiguous problem to credible solution — without losing what matters in between" is the kind of line that someone who actually does this work writes, not someone who outsourced their positioning to an agency. That earns her 60 seconds of my attention.

---

## The credibility problem

I spent three minutes on this site looking for something to bet on. I found very polished positioning and zero evidence.

**No outcomes.** The services section tells me what she produces: use case validation workshops, technical scoping documents, board-level business cases. Fine. What I need to know is what happened after. Did the board approve? Did the programme deliver? Did the company avoid a bad technology decision? The site tells me what she makes — it tells me nothing about whether it works.

**No clients, even anonymised.** Every serious advisory practice — even solo practitioners at the beginning of their career — can point to the type and scale of organisation they've worked with. "A FTSE 100 telecoms group" tells me nothing sensitive but tells me everything about the weight class. There is nothing like that here.

**No numbers.** Not one. "20% IT cost reduction identified" — not there. "Eight-figure programme shaped" — not there. "AI use cases validated across a 3,000-person sales organisation" — not there. The five differentiators are all qualitative assertions about how she works. Not one of them has a number attached.

**The "selectivity" signal works against her at this stage.** "I take on a small number of engagements at any one time" and "Selected engagements only" are designed to create scarcity. But scarcity signalling requires prior reputation to land correctly. If I already knew who Priya was — if I'd heard her name from three people in the telco space — those signals would work. Without prior reputation, they read as either hubris or a consultancy with no pipeline.

---

## Positioning assessment

The dual positioning (AI strategy + IT cost reduction) is smart for the market right now. These are both live problems for CFOs and CTOs at telcos — cost pressure is real and AI pressure is real simultaneously. The fact that she positions across both, with the stated ability to hold both dimensions, is differentiated.

The "ambiguity to output" differentiator is the strongest of the five. That one I believe. The framing around translation is coherent and specific. If I heard her say these things in a room, I would find her credible.

The problem is that I can't hear her in a room. I'm reading a website.

---

## What's missing for investability / referrability

| What I need | What's there |
|-------------|-------------|
| Track record, even anonymised | Nothing |
| Scale signal (org size, deal size) | Nothing |
| One outcome with a number | Nothing |
| A face | Nothing |
| Real contact details | Placeholders |
| Indication of how she works / engagement model | Vague |
| One external voice (client, collaborator) | Nothing |

That table is damning. Not because the site is bad — it isn't — but because everything on it is self-reported. In advisory, self-reported quality claims are table stakes. Clients come from referrals and proof, not from self-reported positioning on a website. This site does not yet create the conditions for either.

---

## What would change my mind

One anonymised case study with a before state, an intervention, and a measurable after state. One sentence from a client. One indication of the scale of organisations she's worked with. Any of these would move me from "interesting" to "worth introducing to someone."

Right now the site says the right things. It just doesn't prove any of them.

---

## Bottom line

I wouldn't pass this URL to a portfolio company looking for a technology advisor — not because I doubt her, but because I have nothing to attach my endorsement to. The site doesn't give me the language to refer her. Fix the proof problem and this becomes a site I'd actually share.
