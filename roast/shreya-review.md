# Shreya Iyer — Brand Strategist Review

**Verdict: Strong voice. No human. Almost there.**

---

## What's working

The copy is the best thing on this site — and I don't say that lightly. "From ambiguous problem to credible solution — without losing what matters in between" is a real headline. It's specific, it creates tension, it earns the second read. Most personal advisory sites lead with something like "I help leaders unlock their potential" — this doesn't, and that's the differentiator.

The voice throughout the About section holds. "The gap was rarely technical capability. The gap was translation." That's a person talking, not a template. The five differentiators are structured without being robotic. The CTA pill that says "Selected engagements only" is a small but smart trust signal.

The dark palette with amber reads as premium and opinionated. Someone made a choice here.

---

## What's broken

**There is no human on this site.**

That is not a small problem. This is a personal advisory practice. The entire product is Priya. People buy advisors before they buy services — and right now the only evidence that Priya exists is that someone wrote some copy. No photo. No face. No sense of what she's like in a room. That pull quote in the About section reads like something someone wrote *about themselves* to sound quotable. It doesn't land the same way a real attributed quote would.

**The pull quote has no source.** "The gap was rarely technical capability. The gap was translation." Said by whom? To whom? In what context? Right now it floats on the page as an anonymous aphorism. Either attribute it (Priya Saira Mistry, to a client, 2023) or reframe it so it reads clearly as her voice rather than hovering ambiguously.

**"Why Priya Saira Mistry" in the nav.** This is awkward. Nobody names their own section "Why [full name]". It sounds like a Wikipedia header. "Why work with me" or just "Approach" would be less self-conscious.

**The five differentiators are all claims.** Point 01: "I hold the business and technical dimension simultaneously." Demonstrated how? There's no story attached to any of them. No "for example" moment. No micro-case. Just five assertions. A visitor who is already sceptical of consultants — which most of the target audience will be — reads these as exactly what they are: a list someone wrote about themselves on a Sunday afternoon.

**The services copy describes what the engagement looks like. It doesn't describe what changes.** "Producing proposals with enough technical depth to survive scrutiny" — but what happened after the proposal? I want outcomes, not deliverables. "The client's IT cost baseline was 40% higher than they thought. We found it in six weeks." That sentence doesn't exist anywhere on this site.

**Would I remember this site?** The headline, yes. The overall impression — polished, dark, amber, consulting — would fade into a category. Nothing anchors it to a specific person doing specific things.

---

## Priority fixes from a brand lens

1. **Add a photo.** This is non-negotiable. Even a well-composed professional headshot at the top of the About section changes the psychological contract entirely.
2. **Add one proof point per differentiator.** One sentence. Anonymised if needed. "A telco leadership team had been trying to align on an AI roadmap for eight months. We got there in two workshops." That's all it takes.
3. **Reframe the differentiators section title.** "Why Priya Saira Mistry" → "How I work" or "The approach."
4. **Source the pull quote** or rewrite it so it clearly reads as her direct voice.
5. **Add outcomes to service descriptions.** Not just "board-level business cases" but "business cases that have secured sign-off."
