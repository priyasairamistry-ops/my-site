# Ankit Verma — Growth Marketer Review

**Verdict: The 5-second test passes. The 30-second test fails. Nobody converts.**

---

## 5-second test

I land on the page. Dark. Amber. "From ambiguous problem to credible solution." I get it: smart consultant, technology, premium. The subheading ("technology advisory for telco, tech, and media") narrows the frame.

**Pass.** I understand the category. I understand the positioning. That's better than 80% of personal advisory sites.

---

## 30-second test — where it falls apart

After the hero, the conversion path disintegrates.

The two CTAs — "Start a conversation" and "See the work →" — are the wrong choices for a first-time visitor. "Start a conversation" implies I'm ready to commit time and context to an outreach I haven't yet justified. "See the work →" links to the Services section, which is not "the work" — it's a description of what she does, not evidence of it. There is no work to see. There are no case studies, no outcomes, no before-and-after, no anonymised client vignettes. The CTA promises proof and delivers a brochure.

The visitor journey is: Hero → About (long) → Services (very long) → Why section (five assertions) → CTA → Contact. **Every section asks me to read more before I've been given any reason to trust.** The page earns nothing before it asks for everything.

## Drop-off mapping

| Section | What happens | Drop-off risk |
|---------|-------------|---------------|
| Hero | Solid value prop, clear CTA | Low |
| About (5 paragraphs) | Dense, no visuals, no proof | **High** — skimmers bounce here |
| Services (3 long cards) | Useful structure but ~600 words each | Medium — useful for warm visitors, loses cold |
| Why section | Five claims, zero evidence | Medium — reads as self-promotion |
| CTA band | Good copy, but third time seeing same CTA | Low incremental value |
| Contact | Form is fine | Low friction — but nobody makes it here |

The page is built for a visitor who arrived already warm (referral, LinkedIn, someone vouched for Priya). For that visitor, it works reasonably well. For a cold visitor who Googled "technology advisor telco AI" — it gives them no reason to stay past the About section.

## Conversion mechanics — what's missing

**No social proof.** The testimonials section was removed. There is nothing on this site that a stranger said about Priya. Not one line. In a world where every consultant claims to be a strategic translator who holds business and technology simultaneously, the absence of a client voice is a conversion killer. Even one real quote — even anonymised — changes everything.

**No secondary conversion path.** The only CTA is "Start a conversation" which goes to the contact form. There is no lower-friction option: no newsletter, no downloadable framework, no "here's a piece of my thinking" — nothing for the visitor who is interested but not ready to reach out. They leave and don't come back.

**The CTA text is passive.** "Start a conversation" is one of the most common and least compelling CTAs in professional services. It implies effort (a conversation requires two people to show up). Compare: "Tell me what you're working on" (curiosity-framed), "Get a straight answer" (outcome-framed), "Let's see if it fits" (low-commitment framing). Any of these would convert better.

**Placeholder contact details are a trust grenade.** `hello@priya.com` — if any visitor clicks that email link and it bounces, or if they try to navigate to `https://linkedin.com` (which goes to the LinkedIn homepage, not a profile) — the credibility damage is severe. These need to be real before a single link is shared.

**No metrics anywhere.** Not even an approximate scale signal. "Organisations" is vague. "Programmes" is vague. One sentence like "across engagements spanning 8-figure IT cost programmes and Series B AI strategy builds" would do significant work. She doesn't need to name a client to establish scale.

**No indication of how to work with her.** What's the minimum engagement? Is she available for a single workshop or does she only take long-term programmes? The "engagements scoped to the problem" line is honest but gives a CFO or CTO no frame for whether this is a £5k conversation or a £500k retainer. That ambiguity stalls decisions.

---

## What I'd test first (if this were a live site with traffic)

1. **Hero CTA** — test "Tell me what you're working on" vs current "Start a conversation"
2. **One testimonial above the fold** — even a pull quote with attribution in the About section
3. **A "How I work" one-pager** as secondary CTA — captures email, continues engagement
4. **Proof element in hero subtext** — add one scale signal: "across 12+ engagements in telco and tech"

---

## Priority fixes from a growth lens

1. **Fix the placeholder email and LinkedIn** — P0. Broken links destroy conversion.
2. **Add at least one testimonial or proof point** — nothing else on this list matters if there's zero social proof.
3. **Replace "See the work →"** with something honest — "Explore services" or remove it.
4. **Rewrite the primary CTA** — something less passive.
5. **Add a secondary conversion path** — a one-pager, a newsletter, anything that captures intent without requiring full commitment.
6. **Add one scale/outcome signal** to the hero subtext or about section.
