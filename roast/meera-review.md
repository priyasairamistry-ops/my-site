# Meera Nambiar — Target Customer Review
*VP of Commercial Operations, large telco. Has budget. Has a problem. Landed here via a LinkedIn referral.*

**Verdict: I'm interested. I'm not convinced. I'm not contacting her yet.**

---

## What happened when I read this

The headline stopped me. "From ambiguous problem to credible solution — without losing what matters in between." That is my situation. Exactly. I have been trying to get our AI strategy off the ground for seven months and every conversation with either the technical team or the board lands differently and nothing sticks. So I read on.

The About section opening paragraph is the second time I nodded: "Most advisory engagements deliver one of two things: a commercially compelling recommendation that does not survive contact with the technical team, or a technically rigorous assessment that cannot land with the business." Yes. That is what keeps happening. I kept reading.

Then I hit the five paragraphs of background. That is where I slowed down. Not because it's badly written — it isn't. But because I'm looking for evidence, and what I'm reading is positioning. I want to know: has she done this specifically? With organisations like mine? With the stakes I'm dealing with? The copy tells me she can do it. It does not tell me she has done it.

---

## The questions I still have

**Who else has she worked with?** Not by name — I understand confidentiality. But scale, sector, type of problem. "A Big Four telco that was 18 months into an AI programme with nothing to show for it" would tell me more than "organisations across telco, tech, and media."

**What does an engagement actually look like?** I'm a VP. I have budget approval authority up to a certain threshold and I need to bring in the CFO above that. Before I even have that conversation I need to know: is this a two-week diagnostic? A six-month programme? A series of workshops? "Engagements scoped to the problem" is the honest answer but it doesn't help me plan internally.

**What did she actually deliver?** "Board-level business cases" — what happened to them? Were they approved? Did the programme land? I need to know that the outputs had consequence, not just that they existed.

**Who is this person?** I cannot find her face anywhere. I'm being asked to start a conversation with someone I cannot picture. Advisory relationships are personal. I need to see that she's a real professional before I put her in front of my CTO.

**Is that email address real?** I hovered over `hello@priya.com` and it felt like a website template. Same with the LinkedIn link going to the LinkedIn homepage. If this is her real site, these should be her real details. If they're not, something feels off.

---

## What would make me contact her today

1. **One client story.** Anonymised completely fine. "A media organisation was three months from a board mandate to reduce its IT cost base by 30%. They had no credible baseline. We built one in four weeks and identified £4.2M in rationalisation opportunities before the board presentation." I would be on the phone that afternoon.

2. **A photo.** I know it sounds superficial. But I'm evaluating whether to bring this person into sensitive internal conversations. I want to see her before I reach out.

3. **A sense of the engagement model.** Even a rough one. "Most engagements start with a structured diagnostic — typically 2–4 weeks — before we agree the shape of the work." That one sentence would reduce my internal selling friction enormously.

4. **One line that acknowledges my specific anxiety.** The biggest risk I face is bringing in an external advisor and then having to explain to my CTO why I bypassed internal teams. If there was a line that addressed how she works *alongside* internal teams rather than around them — that would land.

---

## What nearly put me off

The "Selected engagements only" pill. I understand what this is trying to do — signal exclusivity and premium positioning. But my first read was: what if she doesn't select me? Is this site designed to make me feel like I'm pitching to her? That dynamic can feel right for some buyers. For me, in this moment, it added friction. If I'm going to reach out, I want to feel like she wants to hear from me, not that I'm applying for consideration.

---

## Overall

I believe she's smart. I believe she understands the problem. I do not yet have enough evidence that she has solved a version of my specific problem before. That gap is what's between me and the contact form.
